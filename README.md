 Regional Sales & Revenue Analysis Report

Executive Summary

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of regional sales performance and revenue trends for the first quarter of 2026. The objective was to transform transactional sales data into actionable business intelligence using Pivot Tables to evaluate sales representative performance, product category demand, and payment method preferences.

Dataset Overview

The analysis is based on the primary dataset (Sheet1.csv), which records 16 key transactions between January and March 2026.

Key Data Attributes:

Sales Personnel & Geography: Performance metrics for Sales Representatives (Amit, Priya, Rahul, Sneha) across four major regions (East, West, North, South).

Product Classification: Revenue split between Electronics and Furniture categories.

Financial Metrics: Unit pricing, quantity, and applied discounts.

Transaction Methods: Utilization rates of Cash, Card, EMI, and UPI.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

1. Total Revenue Volume

The total aggregate unit price value for the quarter reached 346,500. This reflects a stable revenue base for the start of the fiscal year.

2. Regional Performance & Leadership

The East region emerged as the primary revenue driver for Q1.

Top Performer: Amit led all sales representatives with a total volume of 114,000.

Regional Rankings: The East region outperformed other territories, followed by the West (103,000), South (81,000), and North (48,500).

3. Category Demand Analysis

Market demand was evenly distributed between the two primary categories, with a slight preference for technology products.

Electronics: Generated 179,000 in total value.

Furniture: Generated 167,500 in total value.

4. Payment Mode Distribution

Digital payment adoption remains high, with traditional methods seeing significantly lower utilization.

UPI: The dominant payment mode, accounting for 142,000 of transaction volume.

Cash: Recorded the lowest preference at 29,500.

5. Quarterly Growth Trend

Revenue showed a positive month-over-month growth trajectory throughout the quarter:

January: 112,500

February: 109,000

March: 125,000 (Peak performance)

Technical Documentation

The following files contain the specific data subsets and pivot logic used for this report:

Sheet1.csv: Master transactional records.

Sheet2.csv: Revenue breakdown by geographical region.

Sheet3.csv: Comparative analysis of product categories.

Sheet4.csv: Individual Sales Representative performance ranking.

Sheet5.csv: Temporal analysis of monthly revenue trends.

Sheet6.csv: Statistical breakdown of payment method preferences.

Conclusion

The data indicates a healthy growth trend peaking in March. Strategic focus should remain on the East and West regions, while the high adoption of UPI suggests continued prioritization of digital payment infrastructure.
